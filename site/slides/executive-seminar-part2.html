HTML5 and Mobile Devices

The problem with "Apps"

Apps are hard to develop

  • Do I start with Android or iOS?
  • If I want both, do I need to hire new people?
  • How long does it take to port?
  • How do I rapidply prototype?

Apps are hard to publish

  • Both iOS Ap Store and Google Play have review process
  • Apple review process can take a week, and have stringent rules
  • Both take revenue cut, around 30%
  • Payments must go through them

Apps create friction

  • Content Discovery? ie GET MY AP FEATURED!
  • Ap sharing involves market search
  • Google doesn't help us by indexing content
  • Must go through installation process
  • Must add to homescreen even before trying ap
  • Too many clicks to get to content, users have to buy in first

HTML5 is better, development

  • Develop for all platforms simultaneously
  • Only need 1 team, who can also do desktop site
  • 10's of millions of web developers, easy to hire
  • Great tools, chrome & firefox
  • Perfect for rapid prototyping

HTML5 is better, publishing

  • Publish and deploy whenever you want
  • You and your users are your only reviewer
  • Select your own payment method, no market to take a cut
  • Same process as desktop website, can use existing infrastructure

HTML5 is better, frictionless

  • No installation process
  • No need to add things to homescreen
  • No need to pay up front
  • Friends can share links (ex Twitter sharing)
  • Google indexes your content, immediately googleable
  • 1 click to get to your content
